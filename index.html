StyleDubber++: Learning Movie Dubbing via Progressive Style-Guided Flow Matching

Abstract: Given a piece of text, a video clip, and reference audio, movie dubbing (also known as Visual Voice Cloning, V2C) aims to generate speech that clones the reference audio while aligning with the video in both emotion and lip movement. Existing methods mainly focus on predicting prosody by fusing face expression features with textual phonemes, and aligning lip-sync via a duration predictor and an external forced aligner. Although significant progress has been made, these approaches suffer from low consistency with target emotion, poor lip-sync accuracy, and the inability to adapt to real-world dubbing scenarios. To address these issues, we propose StyleDubber++, a flow-matching-based framework that advances our prior work across three aspects. First, we introduce a semantic-level emotion adaptor to capture emotion context by mapping multimodal cues into an autoregressive language model for semantic reasoning. Second, we design a progressive style-guided flow matching approach to guide the optimal transport flow trajectory from noise to the target mel-spectrogram by modeling hierarchical conditions spanning basic pronunciation, emotion context, and fine-grained acoustic details. Third, we design a sync-aware vector field estimator with an implicit alignment regularizer that achieves self-supervised audio-visual synchronization and linguistic constraint in latent space to improve lip-sync accuracy. Extensive experimental results on six benchmark datasets demonstrate the state-of-the-art performance of the proposed method. The source code and trained models will be made available to the public.
